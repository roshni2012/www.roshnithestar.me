Chocolates: A Historical Perspective

We all love chocolates.The word chocolate has a very significant place in our minds, be it young or old. But have you ever wondered about the history of chocolate? Yes, you heard it right. The history of chocolate. Just like various other things, chocolates also have a very interesting history. Today, I am going to guide you through a historical perspective of chocolates.

Some significant events in the history of chocolate include:

• The Olmec Indians
Around 1500 BC–400 BC, the Olmec Indians are thought to have been the first to grow cocoa beans as a domestic crop.
• The Mayans
Between 250–900 CE, the Mayans drank an unsweetened cocoa drink made from ground cocoa beans, which was reserved for the elite.
• The Aztecs
The Aztecs believed cacao was a gift from their gods and used it in religious ceremonies. They also used cacao beans as currency.
• Spain
Spain was the first European country to come into contact with chocolate, and it quickly became popular with the nobility.
• Johannes van Houten
In 1828, Johannes van Houten invented Dutch processed cocoa, which is still used today to make cocoa powder.
• The cocoa press
The invention of the cocoa press in 1828 revolutionized chocolate production by making it more affordable and enabling it to be used as a confectionery ingredient.
• The Industrial Revolution
The Industrial Revolution (1750–1850) transformed chocolate production and consumption.
• The first solid chocolate bar
The Fry and Sons shop in England produced the first solid chocolate bar in 1847, and Cadbury introduced their own in 1849.
• Monks and friars
Monks and friars brought home samples of chocolate from missionary work in South America, believing it had medicinal properties.
